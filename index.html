The Office

Capital, Kept
In Order

Titan Meridian Capital exists to do one thing well: steward family capital across market cycles and generations. We are not a product company and we do not manage money for the public. The office concentrates on a small number of mandates executed with institutional discipline.

The estate is deliberately integrated. Research and execution run through Titan Markets LLC, our proprietary trading division in Chicago. Portfolio oversight, reporting, and governance sit here at Meridian — one desk tape, one risk doctrine, one standard of record.

Everything we operate — the terminal, the client portal, the reporting stack — is built in-house, so the family's information stays close and the tooling answers only to the mandate.

I.

Preservation Before Performance

The first duty is to keep capital intact. Return is engineered only on top of survivability.

II.

Process Over Prediction

Positions are the output of written rules — sized, stopped, and reviewed. Conviction is earned by evidence.

III.

One Standard of Record

Every trade, statement, and decision is logged and auditable. Reporting is a discipline, not a courtesy.

IV.

Quiet by Design

We compound in private. Discretion is a structural feature of the office, not a preference.

Mandates

Four Pillars,
One Balance Sheet

The office allocates across four standing mandates. Each carries its own written policy, risk budget, and review cadence — and each reports into the same book.

Public Markets & Trading

Systematic and discretionary trading in FX, metals, index futures, and rates through the Titan Markets desk — TradingView-signaled, broker-executed, risk-gated.

Active · Daily Oversight

Income & Credit

Treasury laddering, cash management, and yield instruments that fund operations and dampen the volatility of the trading book.

Core · Monthly Review

Real & Hard Assets

Physical metals, property, and long-duration holdings — ballast positions held for decades, not quarters.

Strategic · Quarterly Review

Strategic Reserves

Dry powder held for dislocation. Reserves are a position: sized deliberately, deployed on written triggers, replenished on plan.

Standing · Always Funded
The Fund

Meridian
Absolute Return

The flagship internal vehicle. One book, one risk doctrine, executed from the desk and reported to the family with fund-grade discipline.

Risk Doctrine

Fixed fractional risk per position, daily loss limits, weekly drawdown brakes, and an equity-curve circuit breaker enforced in software — the same rules the bot obeys.

Execution

Signals originate in versioned strategy code, route through the webhook layer, and execute at the broker under pre-trade risk checks. Humans supervise; rules decide.

Reporting

Daily desk tape, monthly capital statements, quarterly letters. Every figure traces to the trade log — nothing is quoted that cannot be reproduced.
